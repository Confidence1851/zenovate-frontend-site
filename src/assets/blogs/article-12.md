Among all molecules discussed in the longevity and wellness world, NAD+ (Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide) stands out as one of the most researched and essential. It is a naturally occurring coenzyme found in every cell of the human body.

Globally, NAD+ IV therapy and self-injection protocols have become popular in wellness, anti-aging, metabolic-health, and cognitive-performance programs (especially in Europe, Dubai, Thailand, and parts of Asia).

In Canada and the USA, however, NAD+ injections remain for research use only unless prescribed through specific clinical pathways.

# What Is NAD+?

NAD+ is a vital coenzyme involved in more than 500 cellular reactions, especially those responsible for:

* Cellular energy production

* DNA repair

* Mitochondrial function

* Brain health

* Anti-aging pathways

* Metabolic regulation

As NAD+ levels decline with age, researchers worldwide are investigating whether restoring NAD+ may support healthier aging and improved cell function.

# How NAD+ Works

### 1. Boosts Cellular Energy (ATP Production)

NAD+ is required for mitochondrial energy creation.  
 Higher NAD+ → stronger ATP production → improved energy and metabolic output.

### 2. Supports DNA Repair

NAD+ activates PARP enzymes, which repair damaged DNA — an essential process in aging and longevity research.

### 3. Activates Sirtuins (“Longevity Proteins”)

SIRT1–SIRT7 require NAD+ to function.  
 These proteins regulate:

* Inflammation

* Metabolism

* Stress resistance

* Cellular repair

### 4. Enhances Brain Function

Research suggests NAD+ supports:

* Neuroprotection

* Cognitive clarity

* Mental stamina

* Cellular resilience during stress

This is why international clinics often market NAD+ for focus, clarity, and recovery (not a medical claim).

### 5. Metabolic Support

NAD+ helps regulate:

* Fat metabolism

* Insulin sensitivity

* Oxidative stress levels

Early studies show improved metabolic markers in individuals with low NAD+.

# Why NAD+ Is Used in Global Wellness Clinics

Internationally, NAD+ IV and NAD+ injection programs are incorporated into:

* Anti-aging and longevity protocols

Patients around the world commonly describe:

These are experiential reports — not medical claims.

# Promising Research Findings

### 1. Cellular Repair & Longevity

NAD+ declines ~50% by middle age. Research suggests replenishing NAD+ may support healthier aging processes.

### 2. Mitochondrial Function

Studies show improved mitochondrial efficiency and resilience under metabolic stress.

### 3. Neuroprotection

Early human and animal studies suggest NAD+ may support:

* Neuronal repair

* Cognitive performance

* Stress resilience

### 4. Metabolic Health

NAD+ has been linked to:

* Improved insulin response

* Reduced oxidative stress

* Better lipid profiles

### 5. Addiction & Detox Support

Some wellness centers internationally use high-dose NAD+ IV infusions for detox protocols (research context only).

# Possible Side Effects

Most reported effects internationally are mild and short-lived:

* Head pressure or tightness (common with rapid infusion)

* Flushing

* Nausea

* Fatigue

* Lightheadedness

* Injection-site redness

Side effects typically improve with slower infusion/injection rates.

NAD+ should be avoided by individuals with active cancers due to cellular-growth pathways.

# Anticipated Human Dosing

If approved more broadly in the future, common patterns may resemble:

6 mg once weekly OR 3 mg twice weekly

(Reflecting international practices, *not* medical recommendations.)

# Not Recommended For

* Individuals with active cancer

* Pregnant or breastfeeding individuals

* Those with uncontrolled cardiovascular conditions

* Individuals with severe liver disease

* People with known hypersensitivity to NAD-related compounds
