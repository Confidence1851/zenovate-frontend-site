## What Is NAD+, and Why Is It Important?

NAD+ is a coenzyme found in all living cells. Think of it as the “fuel” that powers essential cellular functions, from DNA repair to energy production. As we age, our NAD+ levels naturally decline—a process associated with aging and an increased risk of chronic conditions. But the good news? Restoring NAD+ levels may help slow down this decline and keep us functioning at our best.

## The Science-Backed Benefits of NAD+

### 1. DNA Repair and Genomic Stability

Our DNA is constantly under attack—from environmental stressors, toxins, and even natural metabolic processes. NAD+ activates sirtuins, proteins that repair damaged DNA and maintain genomic stability. In fact, research suggests that higher NAD+ levels may protect against age-related diseases and enhance overall cellular health.

### 2. Neuroprotection

Brain health is one of the first areas to show signs of aging. NAD+ supports the function of mitochondria—the powerhouses of cells—which are vital for brain energy metabolism. Studies show that NAD+ can help protect neurons from degeneration, potentially slowing the progression of neurodegenerative conditions like Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s.

### 3. Metabolic Health Enhancement

Ever wonder why energy levels plummet as we age? A drop in NAD+ could be to blame. NAD+ plays a crucial role in converting food into energy. Supplementing NAD+ has been shown to improve insulin sensitivity, reduce fat accumulation, and enhance overall metabolic health.

### 4. Anti-Aging and Longevity

Sirtuins, often referred to as “longevity genes,” rely on NAD+ to function. By boosting NAD+ levels, you’re essentially giving these genes the fuel they need to combat aging at a cellular level, from improving skin elasticity to enhancing organ function.
